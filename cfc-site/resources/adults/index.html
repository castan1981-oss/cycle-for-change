Resources · Adults

LGBTQ adult mental health resources

Steady ground for the long haul — a free line that helps you find treatment, directories for therapists who won't make you explain yourself, and peer support that's there whether it's a rough night or a slow grind.

I'm in recovery, and endurance is part of how I stay level — the routine of it, the way a long ride quiets things down. It's not a treatment plan, and I'm not here to sell you one. The orgs below are the real help; the bike is just what carries me to them.

If you're struggling right now: the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is free and 24/7 — call or text 988. For LGBTQ youth, the Trevor Project is at 1-866-488-7386 (or text START to 678-678).

Where to start — LGBTQ adult mental health resources

Crisis · 24/7

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Free, confidential support in any kind of distress — not just the worst moment. Trained counselors, call or text, any hour.

Call or text 988 988lifeline.org →
Treatment referral · 24/7

SAMHSA National Helpline

Free, confidential referrals for mental health and substance use — local treatment, support groups, and community organizations. English and Spanish.

Call 1-800-662-4357 samhsa.gov →
Peer support · trans-led

Trans Lifeline

Peer support run by and for trans people — no nonconsensual active rescue. A line where you don't have to explain the basics first.

Call 877-565-8860 translifeline.org →
Find a therapist

Psychology Today

The widest directory out there — filter for LGBTQ-affirming therapists, your insurance, and how you want to meet. A practical place to start a search.

psychologytoday.com →

Inclusive Therapists

A directory built around safety and access — affirming, culturally responsive providers, with sliding-scale and identity-based search.

inclusivetherapists.com →

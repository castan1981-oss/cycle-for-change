Resources · Young adults

LGBTQ young adult mental health resources

The years where the scaffolding falls away — campus health, a parent's insurance, the friends who knew you. This is help that's yours to keep: affirming therapists, peer lines run by people who get it, and support that doesn't expire at graduation.

For me this was the stretch where structure mattered most. A standing ride, a route I knew, a number that ticked up — it gave the week a spine when not much else did. Movement isn't a substitute for care. It's something to lean on while you find it.

If you're struggling right now: the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is free and 24/7 — call or text 988. For LGBTQ youth, the Trevor Project is at 1-866-488-7386 (or text START to 678-678).

Where to start — LGBTQ young adult mental health resources

Crisis · 24/7

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Free, confidential support for any kind of hard — distress, panic, or just needing a person on the line. Call, text, or chat, any hour.

Call or text 988 988lifeline.org →
Peer support · trans-led

Trans Lifeline

A peer support line run by and for trans people — no nonconsensual active rescue, no police by default. Call when you need someone who shares the experience.

Call 877-565-8860 translifeline.org →
Talk to someone

LGBT National Hotline

Free, confidential peer support for any age — coming out, relationships, isolation, or just a hard week. Not a crisis line; a place to be heard.

Call 1-888-843-4564 lgbthotline.org →

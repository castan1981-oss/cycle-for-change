Resources · Teens

LGBTQ teen mental health resources

If today is heavy, start with the crisis line below — it's free, and you don't need the right words. The rest of this page is for the slower days: people to talk to, and support for when home or school doesn't feel safe.

Riding is part of what kept me steady as a kid who didn't have the words yet. It's not treatment, and it won't fix what's hard — but moving your body is one small lever that's yours. The help on this page is the rest of it.

If you're struggling right now: the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is free and 24/7 — call or text 988. For LGBTQ youth, the Trevor Project is at 1-866-488-7386 (or text START to 678-678).

Where to start — LGBTQ teen mental health resources

Crisis · chat · text

The Trevor Project

Crisis support and a place to talk, built specifically for LGBTQ young people under 25. Call, text, or chat online — any hour.

Call 1-866-488-7386 · text START to 678-678 thetrevorproject.org →
Crisis · 24/7

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Free, confidential support for anyone in distress — not only for the worst moment. Trained counselors, any time, in English and Spanish.

Call or text 988 988lifeline.org →
Peer support · under 25

LGBT National Youth Talkline

Free, confidential peer support from other LGBTQ folks — for the stuff that isn't a crisis but still weighs a ton. Coming out, school, family, just needing to be heard.

Call 1-800-246-7743 lgbthotline.org →

The Jed Foundation

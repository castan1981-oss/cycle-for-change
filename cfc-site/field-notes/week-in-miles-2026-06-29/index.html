Week in Miles: Cycling Phoenix at 100°+

Six rides this week. 224.5 miles total, 6,939 feet of climbing, all of it in Phoenix summer — where the high touched 110°F on Friday and you're done before 9 a.m. or you're cooked.

Phoenix in late June is a negotiation. The heat is non-negotiable — it was above 100°F every day this week. What you can negotiate is timing. I was rolling before 6:15 a.m. on most days. That buys you maybe two and a half hours before the air turns thick and unforgiving. You work within that window or you don't ride.

Wednesday and Thursday were the hardest back-to-back days. The high on Friday hit 110.3°F, and the overnight low on Thursday — the low — was 88.6°F. There's no real cool anymore, just a different shade of hot. I trimmed Friday's ride to 31.8 miles, shortest of the week. That wasn't a failure. That was reading the conditions right. The overcast sky those four days felt almost ironic — no shade cooling, just a lid trapping the heat closer to the ground.

Saturday cleared out and the legs came back. 40.4 miles at 16.9 mph average — the fastest day of the week. Monday morning closed the week with the longest ride, 41.3 miles and 1,565 feet of climbing. Six days, 224.5 miles. Every one of them toward the 7,500-mile pledge. That's what the training looks like right now. Not glamorous. Just done. If you want to follow the miles as they add up, or back the cause, the pledge board is at the bottom of the homepage.

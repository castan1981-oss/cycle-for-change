Cycling in Extreme Heat: 212 Miles, Week of Jun 17–23

Six rides. 212 miles. Six days above 100°F in Phoenix. That is the week of June 17–23, logged mile by mile toward 7,500.

The window to ride in Phoenix is small. June means 6am or nothing. By 8am the heat is a different problem entirely.

June 17 came in sideways — a thunderstorm, 20mph winds, 109°F before noon. Still logged 33.4 miles before the worst of it hit. The legs were stiff. First ride of the week usually is.

No ride June 19. The body needed it. Mid-week settled into something steadier: June 20 and 21 came in clear, 103–104°F, less wind. The miles accumulated without drama. Power output climbed through those days — 137W Thursday, up from 125W Tuesday. The body adjusting, or trying to.

The last two rides hit 16.9mph average, the fastest of the week. The final one, June 23, was logged as "Fun in the sun." Under overcast clouds at 109°F. What is fun about 109 degrees? Getting it done before breakfast. That is the whole game.

Six rides. 212 miles. 7,036 feet of climbing. All of it counted toward 7,500.

Week in Miles: June 30 – July 6

One ride this week — 39.6 miles through Phoenix on Tuesday morning, 100°F high, 1,076 feet of climbing. The summer window is small. The miles count anyway.

One morning ride in Phoenix summer heat

Date Ride Miles Time Avg Speed Climb Weather
Jun 30 Morning Ride 39.6 2h 24m 16.5 mph 1,076 ft Clear · High 100°F · Wind 9.7 mph
Week total 1 ride 39.6 mi 2h 24m — 1,076 ft

Phoenix in early summer means one thing: get out before the heat locks down the day. The low on Tuesday was 75°F at dawn. By the time I finished the 39.6 miles, the high was already at 100.4°F. The ride went out the door at 6:12 a.m. That's the only slot the calendar offers.

The route built 1,076 feet of climbing across the session — not a big day by Phoenix standards, but steady. Average watts sat at 157.5 the whole way. No surges, no disasters. Wind was light: 9.7 mph, clear skies, no rain. The conditions were about as clean as July gets here. You just have to start early enough to finish before the air turns heavy.

One ride doesn't look like much on a spreadsheet. But 39.6 miles is 39.6 miles. The pledge doesn't grade on effort or context — it counts what moves. Every mile is logged live at the tally, and every mile works toward 7,500. If you want to put something behind this work, pledge a mile at /#board.

Week in Miles: Cycling in Phoenix Heat, July 13–20

Four morning rides in Phoenix, July 15–19 — 151.7 miles, 5,033 feet of climbing, and a high of 108.9°F. All four were out before 8 a.m. Every mile logged toward the 7,500-mile pledge for queer communities.

108 degrees on Wednesday

Wednesday was the worst of it. The high hit 108.9°F. The overnight low was 89.8°F — that was the cool part of the day. Phoenix monsoon season brings overcast skies, but overcast here means humid haze, not shade. The heat stays put.

I rolled out at 6:38 a.m. Wind at 17.4 mph, which pushed back on every exposed stretch. Thirty-six miles, 1,165 feet of climbing. The legs were working harder than 15.6 mph suggests. Thursday was a degree cooler at the high — still 99.9°F — and the same wind. Two days back to back, same effort, same early start.

Saturday was the pick of the week

By Saturday, the wind had dropped to under 10 mph and the high only reached 94.6°F. Around here in July, that counts as a good day. Forty-three miles at 17.1 mph average — the fastest of the four rides, and the best I felt all week. When the conditions cooperate even a little, you notice it in the numbers.

Sunday's climbing

Sunday closed out with 2,001 feet of climbing packed into 38.2 miles. The temperature was back up — 103.3°F — and the legs knew what they'd done the four days prior. But it held together. 15.3 mph average, 2h 30m on the bike. The week built from the first ride to the last, which is the point.

Four rides. 151.7 miles. 5,033 feet of climbing. Every one of those miles gets counted toward the 7,500-mile pledge for queer communities. If this week moved you to, pledge a mile and put your name on the next one.

Center Ride Out: LA to San Diego

Three days, roughly 285 miles down the Southern California coast, benefiting the Los Angeles LGBT Center — one of the two rides that carry the AIDS/LifeCycle legacy.

Center Ride Out is the Los Angeles LGBT Center's answer to the end of AIDS/LifeCycle: a three-day supported ride from LA to San Diego, first held in 2026. The Center is one of the largest LGBTQ+ organizations in the world — the ride funds its health, housing, and advocacy programs.

The ride

Expect coastal miles — beach paths out of LA, the Palos Verdes and Orange County coast, the long run through Camp Pendleton toward San Diego. Around 285 miles total, split across three days, with rest stops, mechanical support, and gear transport handled by the event.

Is it for you?

If you can ride 60 comfortable miles in a day, you can train up to this. Three-day rides are the friendliest format in charity cycling: long enough to feel epic, short enough that you don't need a sabbatical. Start with the first-timer's guide and give yourself twelve weeks.
